The UCD Lectures

Between September 1978 and June 1983, I attended lectures at the University of California, Davis as an undergraduate student, a double major in Biochemistry and Philosophy.

From September 1986 to sometime in 1988, I attended lectures at Davis as a graduate (Ph.D.) student in the disciple of Biochemistry and Molecular Biology.

I kept all the lecture notes in a spiral binder, and I eventually scanned in all of the pages, saving the imaged pages in PDF files, contained in this directory.

I have spent a long time transcribing some—but not all yet—of the lecture notes to web documents (files in HTML format).

Why?

Two reasons:

  1. Imaged writing, especially handwriting, is not searchable. Nor is it often legible. Transcribing to readable text is good, and the text now becomes searchable by a keyword.
  2. For my daughter Sequoia, whom I would hope benefit from some of the notes. I have learned that much of what I learned as a student at the university is in fact being taught in Sequoia's high school classes!

Are the notes correct or truthful?

These notes were written down by me. I make no assertion that these are the things that the professor actually said in lecture. In fact the professor of a particular lecture might say he never said these things or that the student (me) misunderstood what was said in lecture. I could have dwelled on a minor point in the lecture whereas the professor wanted to stress a major point.

The notes in the natural sciences should be dated, of course. But facts are facts, even if they are what little we knew more than a generation ago.
