Laws & Government Regulations Related to Medical Device/Product Industry

Glossary: Definitions, Terms

All of the defintions below are verbatim definitions taken from Directive 98/79/EC to harmonize in vitro diagnostic medical devices among the EU member states.

A medical device is any instrument, apparatus, appliance, material or other article, whether used alone or in combination, including the software necessary for its proper application, intended by the manufacturer to be used for human beings for the purpose of:

  • diagnosis, prevention, monitoring, treatment or alleviation of disease,
  • diagnosis, monitoring, treatment, alleviation or compensation for an injury or handicap,
  • investigation, replacement or modification of the anatomy or of a physiological process,
  • control of conception,

and which does not achieve its principal intended action in or on the human body by pharmacological, immunological or metabolic means, but which may be assisted in its function by such means.

An in vitro diagnostic medical device means any medical device which is a reagent, reagent product, calibrator, control material, kit, instrument, apparatus, equipment, or system, whether used alone or in combination, intended by the manufacturer to be used in vitro for the examination of specimens, including blood and tissue donations, derived from the human body, solely or principally for the purpose of providing information:

  • concerning a physiological or pathological state, or
  • concerning a congenital abnormality, or
  • to determine the safety and compatibility with potential recipients, or
  • to monitor therapeutic measures.

Included in the definition are specimen receptacles/containers.

An accessory means an article which, while not being an in vitro diagnostic medical device, is intended specifically by its manufacturer to be used together with a device to enable that device to be used in accordance with its intended purpose. Note: invasive sampling devices or those which are directly applied to the human body for the purpose of obtaining a specimen within the meaning of Directive 93/42/EEC shall not be considered to be accessories.

A device for self-testing means any device intended by the manufacturer to be able to be used by lay persons in a home environment.

A device for performance evaluation means any device intended by the manufacturer to be subject to one or more performance evaluation studies in laboratories for medical analyses or in other appropriate environments outside his own premises.

A manufacturer means the natural or legal person with responsibility for the design, manufacture, packaging and labelling of a device before it is placed on the market under his own name, regardless of whether these operations are carried out by that person himself or on his behalf by a third party. The obligations of this Directive to be met by manufacturers also apply to the natural or legal person who assembles, packages, processes, fully refurbishes and/or labels one or more ready-made products and/or assigns to them their intended purpose as devices with a view to their being placed on the market under his own name. This subparagraph does not apply to the person who, while not a manufacturer within the meaning of the first subparagraph, assembles or adapts devices already on the market to their intended purpose for an individual patient;

An authorised representative means any natural or legal person established in the Community who, explicitly designated by the manufacturer, acts and may be addressed by authorities and bodies in the Community instead of the manufacturer with regard to the latterÃ¢â‚¬â„¢s obligations under this Directive.

The term intended purpose means the use for which the device is intended according to the data supplied by the manufacturer on the labelling, in the instructions for use and/or in promotional materials.

The term placing on the market means the first making available in return for payment or free of charge of a device other than a device intended for performance evaluation with a view to distribution and/or use on the Community market, regardless of whether it is new or fully refurbished.

The term putting into service means the stage at which a device has been made available to the final user as being ready for use on the Community market for the first time for its intended purpose.

The standard ISO 13485 specifies requirements for comprehensive management of design and manufacture of medical devices (definition from Wikipedia); it supersedes EN 46001/46002, and the same standards published before 2003. Generally, product design and manufacture and a system of organization involving design and manufacture should conform to principles in ISO 9001 and/or ISO 13485, and this Quality Management System is certified by a Notified Body. Other requirements may be specified in a EU directive in order to acquire the CE marking.

Directive 98/79/EC

This 37-page directive addresses all definitions, classifications, procedures and standards related to in vitro diagnostic medical devices wanting to be marked CE for sale in the EU member state markets.

There are 35 whereas clauses (in four pages) that address the goals and reasons why the directive is issued, mostly to harmonize the varying standards and regulations among the many member states so that manufacturers can sell throughout the EU.

In a second 10-page section are 24 Articles specifying how member states and manufacturers should govern themselves to make these devices marketable as well as safe and effective.

Then there are 10 annexes in about 20 pages that provide details on the types of IVD medical devices as well as the rules on how to design and manufacture them for the CE marking.

Annex I.A.3 specifies that devices should report analytical and diagnostic sensitivity and specificity, accuracy, repeatability, reproducibility, and control of known relevant interference and limits of detection.

Annex I.B mainly deals with the safety of the device, including those transporting or distributing it, as well as the cleanliness and effectiveness of the device. Annex I.B.8 requires that manufacturers provide information for safe and proper use, taking into account training and knowledge of potential users, and identifying the manufacturer. Generally instructions for use and labeling must be done on a per package basis (a package may contain one or more of the devices). It is probably a good idea to write a Safety section for the user separately from a Proper Use section. The annex recommends use of symbols to inform/warn the reader: use of standard symbols must be done before manufacturer-specific symbols.

When the device is considered dangerous, directives 67/548/EEC and 88/379/EEC specifying how to label and use symbols and how to inform the user. It may be necessary to prepare a safety data sheet (conforming to an MSDS standard).

All package labels must bear:

  • name or trade name and address of manufacturer or authorized representative (in case manufacturer is outside the EU).
  • sufficient detail to identify device and the contents of the packaging
  • use of the word STERILE if that applies
  • use of the word LOT followed by a batch code, or alternatively, a serial number
  • if required, a date by which device/part should be used, by year, month, and day, in that order (the day is not always required)
  • use of performance evaluation only phrase if that is the intended purpose
  • a statement that the device is for in vitro purposes where appropriate
  • storage and/or handling conditions
  • particular operating instruction where applicable
  • any warning and/or precautions to take, where appropriate
  • if intended for self-testing, it must be clearly stated

Instructions for use must contain all the package label details except for any lot/batch code and use-by date. It must also contain

  • reagent product composition nature and amount or concentration of active ingredients of reagent or kit, and statement if other ingredients influence measurement
  • storage conditions and shelf life upon opening primary container, along with storage conditions and stability of working reagents
  • performances specified in I.A.3 (sensitivity, specificity, etc)
  • indication of special equipment (type or specific identification) required
  • type of specimen to be used, special conditions of collection, pre-treatment, and storage; also instructions for preparing the patient
  • detailed description of procedure to be followed for use of device
  • measurement procedure to be followed, including principle of method, specific analytical performance characteristics, limitations of method, details about procedure or handling needed before device can be used (e.g. reconstitution, incubation, dilution, instrument checks, etc), indication of whether particular training is required
  • mathematical approach upoon which calculation of analytical result is made
  • what to do (measures) in event there are deviations/changes in analytical performance of device
  • information related to internal quality control including specific validation procedures and traceability of the calibration of the device
  • any reference intervals for quantities being determined, including description of reference population
  • if device is used in combination or installed with or connected to other medical devices or equipment in order to operate as required, with sufficient details of characteristics to identify the correct devices or equipment
  • all information required to verify whether device is properly installed and can operate correctly and safely, including details of nature and frequency of maintenance and calibration to ensure proper operation; information about safe waste disposal
  • details about treatment/handling needed before device is used (e.g. sterilization, final assembly, etc).
  • instructions in case of damage to packaging and details of re-steriliation or de-contamination
  • for re-usable devices, information related to permit re-use, including cleaning, disinfection, packaging, and re-sterilization and decontamination, and restrictions on number of re-use
  • precuations on exposure to anything potentially harmful or dangerous
  • precautions related to special or unusual risks related to use or disposal of device including protective measures, devices with substance of human or animal origin, potential infectious nature
  • specifications for devices for self-testing (not detailed here)
  • date of issue or last revision for these instructions for use

Annex II contains a list of classes or categories of medical devices. List A specifies reagents and products (incl. calibrators and controls) for determining blood groups (ABO, Rh C/c/D/E/e anti-Kell) and for these infectious agents: HIV-1 and -2, HTLV I and II, and Hep B, C, and D. List B specifies reagents and products for

  • blood groups anti-Duffy and -Kidd
  • anti-RBC antibodies
  • rubella and toxoplasmosis
  • PKU
  • CMV and chlamydia
  • HLA tissue typing: DR, A, B
  • PSA tumor marker
  • trisomy 21
  • blood glucose (blood sugar)

Annex III lists the procedures for obtaining an EC declaration of conformity required before devices can be placed in any market of a EU member state. III.3 is related to technical documentation. It must describe the product generally including planned variants, document the quality system, include design information with determination of characteristics of basic materials, characteristics and limitation of device performance, manufacture method. For instruments it should include design drawings and diagrams of components, sub-assemblies, circuits, etc. For human origin tissues and substances, it must specify collection conditions and origin of material. Descriptions and explanations of characterisitics, drawings, diagrams and operation of product. It should include a result of risk analysis and a list of standards (see Article 5) applied in full or part, and description of solutions adopted to meet essential requirements of the directive. It must include test reports, performance evaluation data claimed by manufacturer and supported by reference measurement system, with information about reference methods, materials, known reference values, accuracy and measurement units used. It must have labels and instructions for use, results of stability studies. III.4 requires description of quality assurance measures: organizational structure and responsibilities in the manufacturer company, process of manufacture and systematic quality control of production, and the means to monitor performance of the quality system. III.5 specifies that systematic procedure which is up-to-date for reviewing experiences gained from devices in post-production phase and implementing means to apply and take corrective actions. A system to notify competent authorities must be in place in case any malfunction, failure, or deterioration of a device

Notified Bodies

The Turkish Standards Institute (TSE) is registered with the EU for certifying compliance with directives on in vitro diagnostic medical devices as Notified Body #1783. It can investigate products in categories IVD 0301 to 0309 to self-testing devices in categories IVD 0401 to 0406. It can process Annexes III (basic EC declaration of conformity), IV (EC declaration of conformity to full quality assurance system), and VII (EC declaration of conformity to production quality assurance).
