Operating Instruction Letter (OIL)

Operating instruction letter (OIL) contains written instructions delivered by Department of Health Care Services (DHCS) to the Fiscal Intermediary (Xerox at the moment) and its subcontractors to request a Medi-Cal policy or system change, or both.

These changes in design or development are focused on an accurate implementation and on timely bulletin changes.

Four Phases

The OIL goes through 4 changes:
  1. Starts with the DHCS indicating that an Request For OIL (RFO) policy change is needed
  2. The CA-MMIS Division of DHCS then writes the OIL
  3. The OIL is implemented by the FI, which is Xerox (EPMO) or its File Maintenance System Group subcontractors
  4. Providers are then notified of the changes by the FI: Xerox's Provider Relations Organization (PRO)

The OIL itself will be a letter with DHCS letterhead with the letter type OIL #XXX-XX where #XXX-XX is a designation assigned when uploaded to SharePoint. The letter must show the following elements:

  • Purpose paragraph
  • History: list of communications relevant or related to the OIL
  • Intent: this is an explanation/justification of the Purpose
  • Description of New Policy or Change in Policy: this is a detailed description of what is to be done in making the changes to the system or policy
  • Update: this specifies what information systems to update and what components are updated
  • Acknowledgment: this may be a statement about how the OIL stays within law or executive order.
  • Publish: this paragraph instructs the FI about what and how to make the changes known to providers or beneficiaries
  • Percentage: This specifies how the federal government (FFP) will participate in the funding of the project as a percentage
  • Contacts + Key Reference Info: This section must indicate several items:
    • Policy Effective Date: usually a month after publication
    • System Implementation Date: usually allowed to be set by FI
    • Policy Originator: the people who determined that policy should generate the OIL
    • OIL Contact: the person responsible for working the OIL through
    • ITMB Analyst: the analyst overseeing the project
    • References: prior communications on the OIL
    • Applies: programs this OIL affects

Examples of Medi-Cal policy and system changes that initiate Phase I (RFO) are maintenance updates to CA-MMIS tables or files or both, or which involve minor programming or compiling of programs or both, to error codes, to the provider manual, or to provider bulletins.

Divisions within DHCS making RFOs include Medi-Cal Benefits Division (MBD), Pharmacy Benefits Division (PBD), Fee-For-Service Rates Development Division (FFSRDD), and others.

CA-MMIS Division receives the RFO. Within the division either the Information Technology Management Branch (ITMB) or the Medi-Cal Policy Evaluation Section (MPES) then prepares the OIL. CA-MMIS may also be told to request provider bulletin or changes to provider manual.

In writing the OIL, the writer will identify if a System Development Notice (SDN) is needed. After writing the OIL, the writer, policy department, and CA-MMIS management must approve fo the letter.
