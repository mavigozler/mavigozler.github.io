A Glossary for Mathematics

This glossary was originally created by its maintainer for his own learning purposes, to keep the maintainer fluent in the terminology and basic concepts important in the understand of computational chemistry. For this reason it is intended that this glossary be authoritative at some level. As such the definitions contained within are always tentative, meaning that they are being revised continually so that the meanings, descriptions, and style of presentation meet the strict and exacting requirements of the maintainer and the readers (namely you), the purpose of which is to make the glossary both readable and informative. Corrections, suggestions, criticisms, and contributions are not only welcome, but they are requested. For all unique contributions or revisions to the glossary, the names of all contributers or revisers will be made a part of this glossary unless inclusion of the name is withheld explicitly by request.

arg
a function which when evaluated represents the phase of a complex number plotted in a complex plane. For the complex number z = x + iy = r cos θ + ir sin θ, the value of arg(z) is θ. Note that r = |z|, which is the modulus of the complex number z.
axiom
a proposition regarded as true in a self-evident manner and without proof; compare to conjecture (hypothesis) in that the statement is true but it is not self-evident
conjecture
  1. a statement that appears to be true but which has not yet been proved (or shown as a proof) or disproved
  2. a conclusion drawn by the inductive process...an educated guess
  3. a proposition consistent with known data, but neither verified nor shown to be false

Synonymous with hypothesis

corollary
  1. a theorem which results from a previously shown theorem that is not shown with rigorous proof
  2. the immediate consequence of a result already proved: an expression of complicated theorem but in a language simpler to use and apply
deductive reasoning
the use of logic (valid argumentation) in order to arrive at conclusion such that, if the premises are true, the conclusion cannot be false in any case; compare inductive reasoning
inductive reasoning
Making general conclusions about some process or property based on the observation of specific examples, identifying trends, and generalizing; it relies on observing patterns but makes no attempt to prove that the pattern applies in all cases; compare deductive reasoning
law
a mathematical statement that is always true empirically and by proof
lemma
a result shown to be true by proof which is used establish a more important result that is a theorem (proposition)
postulate
a statement regarded (taken) as true without a demonstration of proof; postulates are used (as premises) to construct/build/demonstrate/derive lemmas and theorem
principle
a term used broadly in reference to a postulate, theorem, and so on
proof
a rigorous mathematical argument which unequivocally demonstrates the truth of a given proposition, after which the proven is regarded as theorem
proposition
a mathematical statement that will be proved, or shown to be true by construction of a proof, after which it will be regarded as a theorem
theorem
a statement that has been proposed and shown to be true by a proof
