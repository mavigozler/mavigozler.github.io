PCR Methods and Applications, Cold Springs Harbor, 86-88.

Effect of RNA concentration on cDNA synthesis for DNA amplification

What effect does the abundance of message have on the amplification techniques? Primers used for checking for low abundance message were for human c-myc oncogene:

c-myc 5': 1763 GCTTCTCAGAGGTTGG 1779
c-myc 3': 2205 CGTCTAAGCAGCTGCAAG 2188

Primers used for highly expressed message are derived from human and murine g-actin, aligned for maximal homology utilizing Wordsearch and Segments. Jurkat (human T) cells are grown in RPMI-1640 with pen/strep, L-Gln, and 10% FCS present; passed twice weekly. Cells centrifuged (1200 rpm/10 min) and lysed in 4 M guanidine isothiocyanate, 0.5% N-lauroyl sarcosyl, 25 mM Na citrate, and 0.1 M 2-mercaptoethanol. Lysate extracted with phenol, then phenol/CHCl3/isoamyl alcohol, and RNA is precipitated in 50% EtOH. Pellet resuspended in 50 μl diethylpyrocarbonate-treated water, run on gels, measured for amount at 260 nm. Varying amounts used for cDNA synthesis using either 0.5 or 1.0 mM dNTPs: 200-400 units Moloney murine leukemia virus reverse transcriptase in a DTT-containing buffer with 4 units RNase inhibitor (GIBCO/BRL supplies buffer, RTase, inhibitor), and random priming hexamers (0.6 to 1.2 μg), with dNTPs present; reaction preincubated 10 min at 25°, then done 60 min at 42°, then 5 min at 95°, stored −20°.

The cDNA products are then serially diluted and reacted for 30 cycles with c-myc primers accordingly: final concentrations are dNTPs at 0.2 mM, primers at 0.8 μM, and a 1:20 dilution of cDNA if not previously purified of synthesis products (MgCl2 to be added later). Denaturation is to 95° for 5 min, then MgCl2 added to 2.5 mM final concentration, then 1 unit Taq polymerase. Samples are covered with mineral oil, amplified using a program of 3 min at 94°, then 15 cycles of 94° for 1 min (melt), 60° for 1 min, (anneal), 72° for 1 min (elongate). Another 15 cycles are started with 94° for 1 min, 60° for 1.5 min, then 72° for 2 min. Final elongation at 72° for 7 min, cooling to 4° is performed. Reaction is analyzed using 3% agarose gels stained with ethidium bromide and UV illuminated.

Results with the low-abundance message c-myc show that UV intensity varied with the RNA used in the cDNA synthesis reaction, the variation increasing with increasing RNA but leveling off at 10 μg RNA. Densitometric scanning verified the visually apparent results. Altering the amounts of added dNTP and RTase did not appear to cause changes in the amplified DNA levels.

As for high abundance message (g-actin), cDNA dilutions were varied and amplified products measured as before. Different RNA and RTase affected product amounts considerably. 10 μg RNA provided the best reaction and multiple dilution of cDNA worked well (1:1000 worked well). 45 μg RNA was not as effective as 10 μg. These studies indicate that 10-15 μg RNA for both low- and high-abundance messages are the best input for PCR amplification of the product.
