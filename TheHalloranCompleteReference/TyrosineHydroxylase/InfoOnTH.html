Tyrosine Hydroxylase

Structure

Ref.
Tyrosine hydroxylase reported as initial step in norepinephrine synthesis Nagatsu, T., Levitt, M., Udenfriend, S. (1964) J. Biol. Chem. 239, 2910-2917.

A crystal structure1 at 2.3 Å has been characterized which reveals that the Fe atom is surrounded by a basket of alpha helical strands. Also a 40 Å long anti-parallel coiled coil forms the core of the enzyme. The catalytic Fe atom is held 10 Å below the surface in a 17 Å deep well of the active site, held there by two His and one Glu (His-331, His-336, and Glu-376). More interesting is the effect that point mutations in TH and phenylalanine hydroxylase would have on the function of these enzymes and how they might appear in diseased individuals.

1.Goodwill KE, Sabatier C, Marks C, Raag R, Fitzpatrick PF, Stevens RC. (1997) Nat. Struct. Biol. 4,578-85. Crystal structure of tyrosine hydroxylase at 2.3 A and its implications for inherited neurodegenerative diseases.

Reinhard Jr., J.F., Smith, G.K., Nichol, C.A. (1986) Life Sci. 39, 2185-2189.

A working solution of 500 μM [3,5-3H]-L-tyrosine (0.4 Ci/mmol) is prepared from the commercial stock product (usu. 30-50 Ci/mmol). A small volume is taken from the commercial solution and dried under a stream of nitrogen or argon and then reconstituted using unlabeled L-tyrosine. This working solution is discarded. Commercial solutions are discarded after 4-6 months or re-purified using established procedures (see Petrack et al. (1968) J. Biol. Chem. 243, 743).

In a 12 × 75 mm test tube is placed:

  • 5 μmol MES, pH 6.10
  • 2.5 nmol tyrosine HCl
  • 180 μG catalase (Sigma C100)
  • 0.5 μmol DTT
  • 50 nmol 6(R)-L-erythro-5,6,7,8-tetrahydropterin (BH4)
BH4 is added last to start the reaction. Total volume of the reaction should be 100 μL. Blanks are obtained by omitting the addition of BH4. Reactions proceed for 20 min at 37°.

Reactions are terminated by adding 1.0 mL of stirred 7.5% (w/v) Darco G-60 charcoal (J.T. Baker) in 1.0 M HCl. After rapid vortexing for 1-2 seconds and centrifugation at 300-500 x g for 10 min, aliquots of clear supernatant are placed in 10 mL Aquasol II (NEN). Additionally two tubes containing 100 μL of 3H2O (100,000 dpm in each) are treated with charcoal and worked up in the same manner. The fraction of tritiated water recovered is assessed (usually 94%) and is used to adjust the final count. The CPM can be converted to PMOL/MIN by using the following equation:

CPMsample − CPMblank
(CPM added/2 × pmol tyrosine added) (time) (fraction 3H2O)
The optimum of amount of charcoal used for the assay was assessed at 1, 2.5, 5, 7.5, and 10% (w/v). According to the results, a 7.5% value gave the greatest % of triated water recovered while keep the triated tyrosine recovery low. The volume of charcoal used was also varied and 1.0 mL deemed the best. Different charcoal grades were examined: alkaline Norit A was not satisfactory, but G-60 charcoal from Fisher and RIA grade Norit A from Becton-Dickinson were adequate. Assay linearity (r2=0.9929) was compared to the method using Dowex cation column recovery, with results being linear up to 30 min. The blanks as a % of added substrate were compared to the other methods and found to be 0.1% of the total tyrosine added. The separation method is comparable.

Preparation of TH Ser-31 specific kinases from chromaffin cells

Before beginning the procedure, prepare the following:
  1. KRH buffer, warmed to 37°
  2. KRH-KCl buffer, also warmed
  3. Chromaffin cell extraction buffer
  4. DEAE-cellulose equilibration and elution buffers
  5. Mono Q buffers A and B (loading and high salt eluting)
Cultured chromaffin cells are shaken loose from the flask and the medium is removed by centrifugation. Cells are resuspended in KRH buffer warmed to 37°.

Supernatants are recovered and are added to an equal volume of cold DEAE-cellulose equilibration buffer is added. An approximately 10 mL equilibrated DEAE-cellulose column is loaded either by gravity or pump with the supernatant.

The column is washed until OD280 reaches baseline. Elution is by step with DEAE elution buffer again until OD280 is baseline. Fractions of 3-4 mL are collected from this step to minimize pool volume. Pooling is done by looking at the OD280 (protein-containing) fractions. An aliquot of the pool should be saved for peptide activity assay.
