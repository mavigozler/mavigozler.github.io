Notes on Therapeutic Drug Monitoring

Therapeutic drug monitoring (TDM) is used primarily to measure drug levels in drugs with narrow therapeutic index (the margin between desirable effect and the undesirable effect. Propanolol is not toxic even at doses much larger than the dose producing the effect; moreover, its effects can be monitored without lab testing.

Pharmacodynamics

A term used to describe all effects related to a drug, including the unwanted effects.

Pharmacokinetics

The study of a drug's disposition in the body, from absorption, distribution, metabolism, and elimination. The term bioavailability refers to the extent of absorption of a drug reaching the systemic circulation excepting the hepatic portal system. A bioavailability of 1.0 indicates an administration through the IV route, other routes ranging from 0 to 1. A factor used in understanding drug action is the volume of distribution (Vd), which is

Note in these equations, the volume of distribution will determine the drug's extent. Typical value range between 0.25 and 20 liter/kg body weight. Larger volumes indicate a high degree of extravascular tissue binding.

Metabolism & Elimination

Elimination of a drug is usually expressed as a clearance, that is, the volume of plasma completely cleared of drug in a given time (ml/min). After a drug is administered besides its distrubution, a process of altering and eliminating the drug begins. The two-compartment model is used to adequately explain the fate of a drug: one compartment is the intravascular space, the other extravascular. The equilibrium in the extravascular space is slow, the intravascular space immediate by comparsion. Elimination is presumed to occur only intravascularly. Plasma concentration of drug is thought to depend on the rates of drug diffusion due to elimination and distribution to the interstitia, both considered to be first-order kinetics:

Cp = Ae−αt + Be−βt

In the equation α and β are the distribution and elimination rate constants, resp., and A and B are the y intercepts on a plot of Cp vs. time, with A relating to distribution component and B the elimination component.
