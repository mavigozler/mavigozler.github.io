Phosphoprotein & Glycoprotein Silver Staining Protocol

For serial staining, do the phosphate stain first, then follow it with the Moller & Poulsen stain.

Moller & Poulsen Method of SDS-PAGE Gel Staining

[from Anal. Biochem. 226: 371, 1995]

This stain is supposed to be a total protein silver stain that does a better job of staining glycoproteins.

The following steps are performed at 30° with reagents prewarmed:

  1. Fix the gel in 10% trichloroacetic acid for 10 min
  2. Wash gel twice for 2 min each in 5% acetic acid
  3. Incubate gel in 1% periodic acid in 5% acetic acid for 20 min
  4. Wash twice for 2 min each in 5% acetic acid
  5. Wash twice for 2 min each in water
  6. Incubate in 0.5% potassium metabisulfite for 12 min

The following steps are performed at 50° with reagents properly prewarmed:

  1. Wash the gel twice with 25% EtOH + 10% acetic acid for 2 min each wash.
  2. Stain with 0.125% Alcian Blue in 25% EtOH/10% acetic acid for 15 min
  3. Wash the gel three times with 25% EtOH/10% acetic acid for 1, 4, and 5 min in that order.
  4. Continue with the silver staining process, omitting any first fixation steps.

Specific Phosphoprotein Staining

(Cutter & Roth) [Cutting JA, Roth TF (1973). Staining of Phospho-Proteins on Acrylamide Gel Electropherograms. Anal. Biochem. 54: 386-394.

This stain is for phosphoprotein staining. It is modified from Cutting & Roth and is the basis of the Pierce Gelcode Phosphoprotein staining kit. I recommend doing the specific protein stain FIRST, then following it with a total protein silver stain.

For all steps but the heated hydroxide step, agitate (shake) gently.

  1. Preheat an incubation oven at 65°. A water bath produces poor results. Put the 0.5 N NaOH reagent in it to preheat it.
  2. Wash the gel for 10 min in ultrapure water. In all steps the gel must be submerged.
  3. Discard the water and add 10% sulfosalicylic acid for at least 60 min agitating gently.
  4. Discard the 10% SSA and incubate in 10% SSA containing 0.5 M calcium chloride for 1 h or more agitating gently.
  5. Rinse gel with lots of ultrapure water for no more than 30-60 SECONDS to eliminate any CaCl2 on the gel surface.
  6. Quickly remove the discard rinse water. Place gel in 0.5 N NaOH and then in the oven for 20-30 min. There is NO need to agitate/shake the gel for this step. (Note: the gel swells with an increase in 10% of its volume, so use a tray large enough to handle the increase in size. Formation of a white precipitate is normal in this step.)
  7. Rinse the gel in 1% ammonium molybdate for 10 min (use only enough to cover the gel, since this reagent is expensive; recover the reagent in a “used” bottle…you might be able to use it once or twice more).
  8. Repeat Step 7. Note this is done to remove traces of NaOH.
  9. Put gel in 1% ammonium molybdate containing 1 N nitric acid (HNO3) and agitate for 20 min. Recover the nitric acid-molybdate in a “used” bottle for possible re-use.
  10. Place gel in 0.5% methyl green in 7% acetic acid and incubate for 20 min.
  11. Destain with 10% SSA for about 15 min, and repeat the de-stain. Green phosphoprotein bands should be visible against the faint green background.
  12. Continue destaining in 7% acetic acid overnight.

To do total protein staining, wash the gel three times with 100 ml ultrapure water for 10 min each.
