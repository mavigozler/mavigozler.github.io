#### **Question 1: Collision Theory (Conceptual)**
For a chemical reaction to be successful and convert reactants to products, collision theory requires that the colliding particles satisfy which two criteria?
A. High concentration and high temperature.
B. Equal kinetic energy and opposite charges.
C. Sufficient energy and correct geometric orientation.
D. Low activation energy and the presence of a catalyst.

* **Correct Answer:** C. A successful reaction requires the colliding particles to have energy greater than or equal to the **activation energy** ($E_a$) and to be oriented correctly to allow bond breaking and formation.

#### **Question 2: Potential Energy Diagram (Conceptual)**
A potential energy diagram (Reaction Progress Curve) shows that the **products** have a **lower** potential energy than the **reactants**. Which statement is true regarding this reaction?
A. The activation energy ($E_a$) is very large.
B. The reaction is **endothermic**, and $\Delta H$ is positive.
C. The reaction is **exothermic**, and $\Delta H$ is negative.
D. The reaction rate will be slow regardless of the temperature.

* **Correct Answer:** C. Since the potential energy decreases from reactants to products, energy is released into the surroundings, meaning the reaction is **exothermic** ($\Delta H < 0$).

#### **Question 3: Activated Complex (Conceptual)**
In a potential energy diagram, the peak of the "hump" represents the **activated complex**. Which of the following best describes this species?

D. It is an unstable, energetically excited **transition state** structure that either proceeds to products or reverts to reactants.

* **Correct Answer:** D. The activated complex is the highest-energy point along the reaction coordinate, representing a transient, unstable structure in the process of forming or breaking bonds.

#### **Question 4: Reaction Rate Factors (Conceptual)**
Increasing the **concentration** of reactants in an aqueous solution will generally increase the reaction rate. This is primarily due to an increase in which factor?

* **Correct Answer:** B. Higher concentration means more particles are packed into the same volume, which increases the likelihood (frequency) of successful collisions.

#### **Question 5: Catalysis (Conceptual)**
A catalyst affects the rate of a chemical reaction by:
