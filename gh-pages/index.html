Goals and Methods

The goal of tutoring is to help you, the student, with succeeding in courses you are taking at an educational institution. It is not to offer the course work itself apart from the objectives of the school or college instruction you are getting. Find out more on how tutoring preparation is done.

In-Person or Online

Online tutoring is occurring more and more these days, and there are many advantages.

  • Right at Home. You don't have to leave home if you don't want to.
  • Security. Because you don't have to leave home and invite someone you consider a relative stranger into your home, there's no worry or need for background checks, if this is important to you
  • Lower Cost. There is no cost for travel at all, both its time and resources like fuel and vehicle wear-and-tear

Some students are not comfortable with online interaction however, and feel they get a better interaction through a face-to-face session. Decide what is best for you.

One-on-One or Group

Your best result would be a completely one-on-one session with your tutor. But if there are a group of you studying the same course/subject/content, I can teach a group of up to 6 learners. I will not teach more or else I would probably just open up a school, and so teaching larger numbers is impractical unless you really want a school.

Because the time and attention are divided among more persons in the group, There is a discount on the per hour rate for each student. I could not divide the rate by the number of each student because that can affect the incentive to learn in a more one-on-one interaction. See the table below on group rate calculation.

First Session

I prefer the first session be only 30-45 minutes long. I expect the first session actually to be a "get acquainted" session and to get organized. I will not charge a fee for any session that is not related to actual tutoring/learning, but which are just administrative things like this.

Setup/Resources Recommendations for Online Tutoring

  • Computing device
    • Desktop: 21- to 24-in monitor recommended as minimum
    • Laptop: 15.6 in monitor
    • At least 1920 x 1080 (Full HD) resolution
    • Audio/video: a 720p webcam is recommended, and a microphone built-in to monitor or headset
    • A dual-core 4 GB RAM is a minimum, and 8 GB quad core is recommended
    • While a keyboard & mouse will work, and added benefit is obtained if a touchscreen or Wacom tablet is helpful
  • Internet Connection
    • Bandwidth: Your download service should have a minimum 5 Mbps, but 20 Mbps is really best for HD video calls & whiteboarding
    • Your latency (ping) should be less than 100 ms, but dont worry if you cant determine it
    • If a wireless connection to computer to home LAN is used, make sure it does not contribute to any lag
    • Learn whether the ISP throttles service: a 1-2 GB per hour connection is needed typically if whiteboarding
