Mechanisms of Translational Control

These notes (by S. M. Halloran) are of lectures delivered and course materials distributed by Dr. John W. Hershey, professor of the Department of Biological Chemistry in the medical school of the University of California, Davis; given in winter quarter (trimester) of 1987-1988 academic year.

11 January 1988

Lecture 1: Theory and Methods.

Useful references:

  • Moldave, K. (1985) Ann. Rev. Biochem. 54, 1109-1149. Eukaryotic Protein Synthesis.
  • Pain, Y.M. (1986) Biochem. J. 235, 625-637. Initiation of Protein Synthesis in Mammalian Cells. Mathews, M.B., ed. (1986) Current Communications in Molecular Biology: Translational Control, Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory, New York.

The following papers are assigned for reading to be discussed in two weeks:

  • Fan, H., and S. Penman (1970) J.Mol.Biol. 50, 655-670. Regulation of Protein Synthesis in Mammalian Cells. I. Inhibition of Protein Synthesis at the Level of Initiation during Mitosis.
  • Palmiter, R.D. (1975) Cell 4~, 189-197. Quantitation of Parameters That Determine the Rate of Ovalbumin Synthesis.
  • Chin, K-Y., C. Cade, C.O. Brostrom, E.M. Galuska and M.A. Brostrom (1987) J. Biol. Chem. 262, 16509-16514. Calcium-dependent Regulation of Protein Synthesis at Translational Initiation in Eukaryotic Cells.
