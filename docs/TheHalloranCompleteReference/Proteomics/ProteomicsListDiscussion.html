The Proteomics List Discussion Group

  • Perhaps you are working in some part of proteomics and wish that you could get past the frustration of working with a method or piece of equipment or software that is troubling you.
  • Perhaps there is no one to turn to for advice or help.
  • Perhaps you have a lot of experiences and knowledge you wish to spread around.

If any and all of it has to do with proteomics, then there may be a forum just right for you.

Subscribe now to the Proteomics email list discussion group.

You can subscribe:
  • by email: Put in the Subject: header "subscribe" and send it to proteomics-request@freelists.org (or just click that link, and it’s all done for you)
  • by using web forms interface at FreeLists and at the same time configure the many options for participating in the list.
You require:
  • no special qualifications
  • no advanced degrees or certificates
  • no memberships in honorary organizations
  • the ability not to be afraid to introduce yourself and not be afraid to ask questions
The only (other) requirement is an interest in proteomics.

If you want, lurk for a while and just read what is posted. You can probably learn a lot just by lurking and reading.

We encourage but do not limit subscription for the following types of people:

  • Individuals who call themselves proteomists (is that a word?) because they devote their daily activities to nothing but proteomics or, in their typically saintly fashion, helping others to do proteomics
  • Investigators, scientists, academic professionals and students of proteomics or who make use of proteomics as a tool in their work. There is no restriction on what type of institution or organization you might work for, public or private
  • Support professionals, including sales and technical support personnel of products used in proteomics
  • Equipment manufacturers, software developers of proteomics-related products

Product Naming, Advertising, Spam

The list allows the naming and promotion of products related to proteomics with certain limitations (e.g., subject headers must say they are ads to give subscribers the option of disregarding or filtering them out). We also encourage our subscribers to say bad things about products if they believe it to be the truth. So if you sell the product, you might want to sign up to defend it or explain why it is not being used properly.
