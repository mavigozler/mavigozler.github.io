A Glossary for Computational Chemistry

This glossary was originally created by its maintainer for his own learning purposes, to keep the maintainer fluent in the terminology and basic concepts important in the understand of computational chemistry. For this reason it is intended that this glossary be authoritative at some level. As such the definitions contained within are always tentative, meaning that they are being revised continually so that the meanings, descriptions, and style of presentation meet the strict and exacting requirements of the maintainer and the readers (namely you), the purpose of which is to make the glossary both readable and informative. Corrections, suggestions, criticisms, and contributions are not only welcome, but they are requested. For all unique contributions or revisions to the glossary, the names of all contributers or revisers will be made a part of this glossary unless inclusion of the name is withheld explicitly by request.

matrix
A rectangular array of mathematical elements, having row and column structure and described as being a m × n matrix having m rows and n columns; the mathematical elements often describe the properties or structure, or both, of real systems (system might be motion or position of objects in physics, or of reaction or energetic properties in chemistry, or of forces in engineering), and upon which operations both basic (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division) and advanced can be done to obtain additional properties (solutions) those systems, if correctly described by the matrix
Rayleigh scattering
The scattering of light by particles which are much smaller than the wavelength of the light. It is characterized by a scattered intensity which varies as the inverse fourth power of the wavelength. [CRCHCP]
scalar
any quantity having a magnitude typically expressed a real number

a matrix having an equal number of row and column elements, or a m×n matrix where m=n; operations can be performed on a square matrix that cannot be performed on a matrix where m≠n
vector
a mathematical description of an object having a magnitude and a direction with reference to an n-dimensional coodinate system and its origin
